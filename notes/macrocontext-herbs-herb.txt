## A. Identity Attributes (Entity Definition Layer)

This establishes **clear entity boundaries**.

**Subtopics**

* Scientific name (binomial)
* Plant authority (taxonomy authorship)
* Common names

# 4. Microsemantic Tip (Critical for Monographs)

Your **first paragraph must disambiguate the entity instantly**:

> *Echinacea purpurea* is a flowering plant species in the Asteraceae family used in herbal medicine for its immunomodulatory properties. Native to North America, it is commonly prepared as tinctures, teas, and extracts from its roots and aerial parts to support immune function and treat respiratory infections.

This does 4 things immediately:

* defines the **entity (species-level)**
* connects to **parent entity (medicinal plant)**
* introduces **core attributes (compounds, uses, forms)**
* reduces **ambiguity vs similar species**

# 5. Advanced Koray Tip (What Most Miss)

For each monograph, include **3 represented queries + query path**:

Example:

```
Query 1: What is Echinacea used for?
→ How does Echinacea support the immune system?
→ Is Echinacea effective for colds?

Query 2: Is Echinacea safe?
→ What are Echinacea side effects?
→ Can Echinacea interact with medications?

Query 3: How to take Echinacea?
→ Echinacea dosage for adults
→ Best form of Echinacea (tea vs tincture)
```

This creates **query coverage depth**, which directly improves:

* passage retrieval
* featured snippet eligibility
* AI answer inclusion
