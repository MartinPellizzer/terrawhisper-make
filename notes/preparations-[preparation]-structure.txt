#### **Section 1: The Semantic Definition (The "What" Layer)**
*   **Goal:** Establish the Entity and its place in the hierarchy.
*   **Intent:** Informational (Definition/Breadth).
*   **Attributes to Cover:**
    *   **Formal Definition:** A clear, scientific-style definition of the [Entity].
    *   **Classification:** Where it sits in the spectrum of herbalism (e.g., "A water-based, low-heat extraction").
    *   **The Core Purpose:** Why this specific method exists (e.g., to preserve delicate volatiles vs. to break down tough cell walls).

#### **Section 2: The Solvent Dynamics (The "Mensstruum" Layer)**
*   **Goal:** Define the medium that carries the active constituents.
*   **Intent:** Informational (Technical/Attribute).
*   **Attributes to Cover:**
    *   **Solvent Identity:** (Water, Alcohol, Glycerin, etc.).
    *   **Solvent Properties:** Polarity, boiling point, pH, or concentration (ABV).
    *   **The Interaction:** How this specific solvent interacts with plant matter (e.g., "Water's ability to dissolve polar molecules").

#### **Section 3: The Botanical Matrix (The "Material" Layer)**
*   **Goal:** Define the compatibility between the solvent and the plant part.
*   **Intent:** Informational (Selection/Criteria).
*   **Attributes to Cover:**
    *   **Ideal Plant Tissues:** (Roots, barks, leaves, flowers, resins, or seeds).
    *   **Tissue Characteristics:** (Soft/delicate vs. Hard/fibrous).
    *   **Preparation of Material:** (Dried vs. Fresh, or the required grind fineness).

#### **Section 4: Phytochemical Targeting (The "Chemistry" Layer)**
*   **Goal:** Explain *why* this method works at a molecular level. This is the "Authority" section.
*   **Intent:** Informational (Scientific/Depth).
*   **Attributes to Cover:**
    *   **Targeted Constituents:** Which specific compounds are extracted (e.g., Alkaloids, Tannins, Flavonoids, Mucilage, or Essential Oils).
    *   **Solubility Logic:** Why the chosen solvent is required for these specific molecules (e.g., "Alkaloids are best extracted via alcohol due to...").
    *   **The Extraction Mechanism:** (Diffusion, Osmosis, or Thermal breakdown).

#### **Section 5: The Extraction Protocol (The "Procedural" Layer)**
*   **Goal:** Provide the actionable "How-to" instructions.
*   **Intent:** Instructional (Procedural/Quantitative).
*   **Attributes to Cover:**
    *   **Variables of Extraction:** (Temperature, Duration, Ratio, and Agitation/Shaking).
    *   **Step-by-Step Methodology:** A precise, logical workflow.
    *   **Quantitative Ratios:** (e.g., 1:5 weight-to-volume ratio, or grams per ml).

#### **Section 6: Stability, Safety, and Storage (The "Reliability" Layer)**
*   **Goal:** Address the lifecycle of the prepared entity.
*   **Intent:** Informational (Safety/Maintenance).
*   **Attributes to Cover:**
    *   **Shelf Life/Stability:** How long it lasts and what causes degradation (light, oxidation, evaporation).
    *   **Storage Requirements:** (Amber glass, cool/dark place, airtight seals).
    *   **Safety/Contraindications:** (e.g., "Alcohol-free options for children" or "Tannin-heavy decoctions and stomach irritation").

#### **Section 7: Comparative Context (The "Relational" Layer)**
*   **Goal:** Connect this node to the rest of your Knowledge Graph.
*   **Intent:** Comparative (Relational/Navigation).
*   **Attributes to Cover:**
    *   **[Entity] vs. [Alternative]:** (e.g., "How this differs from a Decoction").
    *   **When to Choose This Method:** A summary decision-making guide (e.g., "Use a Tincture when you need long-term stability; use an Infusion for immediate, gentle use").
